<p>&nbsp;by Michael K. &#8211; Scorpions &amp; Eagles historian report</p>
<p>&nbsp;The Old Rag hiking outing was very long and had lots of great scenery.&nbsp; Patrols hiked many miles to reach the top of the highest mountain.&nbsp; Before we even left the church however, we had to shovel a gigantic pile of mulch blocking the trailer’s path.&nbsp; This delayed the leaving time by about 20 minutes&#8230;</p>
<p><span id="more-4560"></span></p>
<p>&nbsp;but we still arrived at Old Rag at an exceptional time.&nbsp; The Scorpions patrol combined with the Eagles patrol, and together we hiked at least eight miles and reached the top in about four hours.&nbsp; The terrain was super rocky, and the paths were very steep at points.&nbsp; At the start, the path had a slight incline and was semi rocky.&nbsp; As we advanced higher up the mountain, the path got steeper, and the terrain got rougher.&nbsp; The weather got cloudier and windier as we continued.&nbsp; Eventually, Joe’s glasses flew off his head after jumping onto a rock.&nbsp; Gordon and I retrieved them about 20 meters away from Joe in the middle of two very large rocks.&nbsp; About ¾ of the way, the clouds started clearing, but the wind continued to be a hazard.&nbsp; Once we got to the top, the temperatures were freezing, combined with the frigid wind-chill.&nbsp; We took a VERY long fire route down the mountain, where branches were flying everywhere because of the strong winds.&nbsp; Gordon and I saw at least three small trees fall, and one big one almost hit a tent near a river.</p>
<p>&nbsp;<a href="/wp-content/uploads/2017/01/OldRag-2016-5.jpg"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-4567" src="/wp-content/uploads/2017/01/OldRag-2016-5-279x300.jpg" alt="OldRag-2016-5" width="279" height="300" srcset="/wp-content/uploads/2017/01/OldRag-2016-5-279x300.jpg 279w, /wp-content/uploads/2017/01/OldRag-2016-5.jpg 409w" sizes="(max-width: 279px) 100vw, 279px" /></a>&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;&nbsp;<a href="/wp-content/uploads/2017/01/OldRag-2016-3.jpg"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-4569" src="/wp-content/uploads/2017/01/OldRag-2016-3-300x189.jpg" alt="OldRag-2016-3" width="300" height="189" srcset="/wp-content/uploads/2017/01/OldRag-2016-3-300x189.jpg 300w, /wp-content/uploads/2017/01/OldRag-2016-3.jpg 692w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a></p>
<p>When we got back to the campground, the entire troop decided to settle in the barn overnight.&nbsp; Before we went to sleep, we all cooked dinner right outside the barn.&nbsp; The Scorpions had delicious tacos, with perfectly cooked ground beef.&nbsp; I had no idea what the dragons were making, but it looked nasty.&nbsp; When it was finally time to rest, &nbsp;everyone set up tents inside the barn since the temperatures were freezing.&nbsp; We even had christmas lights inside the barn for the true campout experience.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>We had a rough start the next morning.&nbsp; The cold of the morning kept everyone from wanting to do anything.&nbsp; Eventually, everyone woke up and made breakfast.&nbsp; The day was semi sunny with many clouds still in the area.&nbsp; There were gales every now and then, which made it especially hard to cook breakfast.&nbsp; The troop left the place around the time we expected to, which is very good.&nbsp; The ride home was long, but everyone was super happy when we finally got into warmer temperatures.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>I would definitely go on the outing again with the troop in warmer and less windy temperatures.</p>
