<p style="text-align: left;">September 2016 Rappahannock&nbsp;River Canoe Outing &#8211; By Jacob Hochstein (Vikings Patrol)</p>
<p><a href="/wp-content/uploads/2016/11/Sept2016-Vikings-historian-2.jpg"><img decoding="async" class=" wp-image-4384 size-medium alignleft" src="/wp-content/uploads/2016/11/Sept2016-Vikings-historian-2-300x169.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="169" srcset="/wp-content/uploads/2016/11/Sept2016-Vikings-historian-2-300x169.jpg 300w, /wp-content/uploads/2016/11/Sept2016-Vikings-historian-2-1024x576.jpg 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" /></a></p>
<p>The September canoe outing was great fun.&nbsp;&nbsp;We stayed in a camp ground near the river that has been persevered for campers such as our troop for over 45 years.&nbsp;&nbsp;Although we very near Fredericksburg it felt like we were in the deep wood.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><span id="more-4380"></span></p>
<p>While paddling on the Rappahannock River we saw many sights including fish, turtles and muskrats.&nbsp;&nbsp;Paddling was fun as well.&nbsp;&nbsp;Along the way, we went swimming and had a great time.&nbsp; I think we&#8217;re ready for some white water next time.</p>
<p>That evening while cooking everyone was having fun.&nbsp; The Vikings dessert may have failed miserably but it was fun trying to boiling donuts in a Dutch Oven.&nbsp;&nbsp;A pineapple upside down cake won the cooking competition.&nbsp;</p>
