<p style="text-align: left;"><strong>Great Falls Freedom Memorial&nbsp;&#8211; May 29, 2017</strong></p>
<p>by Cam Beem&nbsp;&#8211; Vikings Patrol</p>
<p>Some volunteering scouts from the troop went to the Memorial Day service on May 29, 2017. &nbsp; First, everybody helped set up the chairs for the guests. Once that was finished, we did some practice runs of what we were going to do during the service. &nbsp;After that, the service started. We listened to all of the inspiring speeches and songs, which made us proud to be Americans. &nbsp;The keynote speaker was Robert D. Vickers Jr. who spoke about his service in Vietnam.&nbsp;</p>
<p><span id="more-4790"></span></p>
<p>When the service was done, we took a group picture.<a href="/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/Memorial-Day-2017-2.jpg"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="  wp-image-4793 alignleft" src="/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/memorial-Day-2017-3.jpg" alt="memorial Day 2017-3" width="401" height="301"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4792" src="/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/Memorial-Day-2017-2.jpg" alt="Memorial Day 2017-2" width="206" height="274"></a>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Once that was over, we put away the chairs. &nbsp;We also got a picture with the Marines who were there.&nbsp; They did the color guard and played taps to honor those from Great Falls who have lost their lives in service.</p>
