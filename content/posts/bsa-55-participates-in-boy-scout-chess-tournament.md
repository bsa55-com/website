<p><strong>1st Annual Boy Scout Akela Chess Classic – February 11th, 2012</strong></p>
<p>On February 11th the 1st Annual Boy Scout Akela Chess Classic was held at Potomac Baptist Church in</p>
<div id="attachment_372" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption alignright"><a href="/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/Boy-Scout-Chess-1.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-372" decoding="async" class="size-medium wp-image-372" title="Boy Scout Chess 1" alt="February 2012 Chess Tournament" src="/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/Boy-Scout-Chess-1-300x225.jpg" width="300" height="225"></a><p id="caption-attachment-372" class="wp-caption-text">February 2012 Chess Tournament</p></div>
<p>Lowe’s Island, VA. Organizer and sponsor Roger Claff decided to create a tournament for Boy Scouts&nbsp;this year after having had so many successful Cub Scout tournaments. In addition, the new Chess merit&nbsp;badge approved in 2011 has an optional “tournament” requirement and the Akela Classic provides the&nbsp;boys with an excellent opportunity to fulfill that requirement and make headway toward this unique&nbsp;merit badge.</p>
<p>Twenty-one Boy Scouts participated in this year’s tournament, most of them Cub Scout alumni from&nbsp;years past. The scouts were split into two age groups, 10-12 and 13 and above. The tournament was&nbsp;run in the classic “Swiss” style of tourney play. Each scout played in each of the 5 rounds, receiving 1&nbsp;point for a win and a half-point for a stalemate or draw. The tournament was “touch-rule” meaning&nbsp;if you touch a chess piece you must move that piece. This is often challenging for scouts who are not&nbsp;familiar with this style of chess play.</p>
<p>Six scouts from Troop 55 competed in the tournament: Liam G. (Bears), Trey M. (Bears),&nbsp;Daniel D.(Bears), Daniel R. (Dragons), Joshua O.(Bucks) and Tim F. (Sharks). Four&nbsp;of the scouts also brought home trophies: Daniel D. 2nd Place 10-12 division, Trey M. and Tim&nbsp;F. 3rd Place in the 10-12 division and Liam G. 3rd Place in the 13 and older division. A superb&nbsp;showing from Troop 55!</p>
<div id="attachment_373" style="width: 310px" class="wp-caption alignleft"><a href="/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/Boy-Scout-Chess-2.jpg"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-373" decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="size-medium wp-image-373" title="Boy Scout Chess 2" alt="Action Shot of Chess Competition" src="/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/Boy-Scout-Chess-2-300x225.jpg" width="300" height="225"></a><p id="caption-attachment-373" class="wp-caption-text">Action Shot of Chess Competition</p></div>
<p>Many of the boys also recorded their games and replayed them with the tournament co-host and chess&nbsp;instructor, Peter Snow, to fulfill one of the Chess Merit badge requirements.</p>
<p>Mr. Claff, ardent scouter and chess proponent, closed the tournament with a personal anecdote on&nbsp;learning chess from his father in 5th grade and how his father “cleaned the board up with me” but he&nbsp;never gave up and kept learning, practicing and playing. Claff encouraged the boys to keep trying and&nbsp;keep playing and said that he hoped he’d see us all at next year’s tournament.</p>
