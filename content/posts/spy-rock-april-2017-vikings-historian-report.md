<p style="text-align: left;">&nbsp;<strong>Spy Rock &#8211; April 2017</strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;by Cam Beem&nbsp;&#8211; Vikings Patrol</p>
<p>This April, the Viking patrol (notice there is no plural, it was just me from the Vikings) had a great time backpacking on the Appalachian trail, seeing the great views of Crabtree Falls and Spy Rock. &nbsp;The car ride was very long, and I had nothing to do for three hours but it was fine. When I got there, we did quite a bit of waiting because the Feng-Mobile (Mr.Feng’s Car) took forever, so instead of having lunch at the top, we had lunch at the bottom of the mountain before we began our hike.</p>
<p>After an hour and a half or so of nonstop switchbacks, which are never easy, we came to the top of Crabtree Falls, which looked something like this&#8230;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;<span id="more-4775"></span></p>
<p>The view was great, and we ate some snacks because we had already feasted on our lunches meant for this spot. <a href="/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/SpyRock-2017-Vikings1.jpg"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="  wp-image-4777 alignright" src="/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/SpyRock-2017-Vikings1-225x300.jpg" alt="SpyRock-2017-Vikings" width="140" height="187" srcset="/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/SpyRock-2017-Vikings1-225x300.jpg 225w, /wp-content/uploads/2017/08/SpyRock-2017-Vikings1.jpg 445w" sizes="(max-width: 140px) 100vw, 140px" /></a></p>
<p>So we went on, and hiked for about three or four hours, which were hard to count I’m just guessing, and we came to a stop at the same clearing we camped in last year, and something amazing happened! &nbsp;Last year, a piece of equipment for one of our backpacking stoves went missing, and we found it here, and in perfect working shape! It was quite the finding!</p>
<p>After a nice dinner, we went to bed. A few people texted their parents because we had cell service in that particular spot in the mountain.</p>
